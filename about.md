# About SmartMoneyDaily

## Who writes here

My name is **Kkuma Park**. I'm an independent writer and developer based in Seoul, South Korea, with more than a decade of experience in IT. I started SmartMoneyDaily because most explanations of savings accounts, CDs, and money market accounts are either oversimplified or buried in bank marketing — and I wanted a plain-English reference I could actually trust. You can find my open-source work on [GitHub](https://github.com/Kkumakkuma), where this site is also built and published in the open.

## What SmartMoneyDaily is for

SmartMoneyDaily focuses on one thing and tries to do it well: helping U.S. readers understand and compare **high-yield savings accounts (HYSAs), certificates of deposit (CDs), and money market accounts**.

You'll find explainers on how APY and compounding actually work, how FDIC and NCUA deposit insurance is calculated, how CD early-withdrawal penalties work, and how to compare accounts without being misled by a single headline rate.

## How I keep this accurate

- **I stick to verifiable, public information.** Where rules and figures matter, I rely on primary U.S. sources such as the FDIC, the Federal Reserve, the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB), the NCUA, and the U.S. Treasury — and I name them in the article so you can check.
- **I don't quote a "current rate" as if it were fixed.** Interest rates change constantly, so instead of pretending to know today's exact APY, I explain how to find and compare up-to-date rates yourself.
- **I use AI tools to help draft and structure articles, and I curate the topics and check them against public sources before publishing.** I'd rather be transparent about that than claim a personal test I didn't run.
- **I correct mistakes.** If I find that an earlier post was inaccurate or out of date, I fix it.
