## How Do Gold ETFs Work?

Gold ETFs are investment funds that aim to replicate the price movements of gold by holding the actual commodity or gold futures contracts. Here's how they operate:

1. **Ownership of Gold**: Many gold ETFs hold physical gold bullion, while others invest in gold futures. SPDR Gold Shares (GLD), for instance, tracks the price of physical gold by holding gold bars in secure facilities.
  
2. **Trading on Secondary Markets**: Gold ETFs are traded on major stock exchanges like shares of stock. Investors can buy and sell throughout the trading day, giving them liquidity.

3. **Expense Ratios**: These funds charge a small fee, known as an expense ratio, which typically ranges from 0.2% to 0.5% annually. This is much lower compared to the costs associated with owning physical gold.

4. **Dividends**: Unlike stocks, gold ETFs typically don’t pay dividends, as the fund's value lies solely in the price of gold increasing.

Understanding these elements is crucial for anyone looking to invest. As I learned, trading in gold ETFs can be far more convenient than managing physical gold.
